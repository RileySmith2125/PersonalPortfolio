I really enjoyed this book. However, it's definitely written for soccer fans, not data fans. The biggest shortcoming of the book, to me, was that it didn't dive deeper into the analysis or methods. In this way, the title "How to Win the Premier League" is misleading, the book does not really give you much "how". It's much more of a recounting of events in the soccer world through the author's data-driven eyes. I'd also warn potential readers that there is a lack of cohesiveness in the book- there is a fair bit of jumping around between topics and times. Ultimately the author develops some themes and lessons which I appreciated, but he rarely spends an extended amount of time building a particular argument, narrative, or scene.

Again though, I enjoyed this book a lot and found the author's opinion to be interesting and convincing throughout. It's clearly written by someone who is both a soccer fan and a scientist, and those two voices are balanced well in the author's opinions, even if the book doesn't dive into the science deeply.

Also, this book made me better at Fifa.
